Manifesto of the Peaceful Occupation

At the time of writing, the State of Israel is committing ethnic cleansing. Since the beginning of its siege, the Gaza Strip has been cut off from deliveries of food, water, fuel, medicine and humanitarian aid. As a result of bombing and shelling, hospitals, universities, schools and other civilian infrastructure in which the Palestinian population could seeks shelter are being continuously razed. As a result of the Israeli government's unimaginable violence and cruelty, the death toll, as well as the number of displaced and evicted Palestinians is increasing every day. Faced with these events, we believe that it is our ethical and moral responsibility to stand in categorical, resolute and conscious solidarity with the Palestinian people, and that this warrants specific action. A week has passed since the rector of the UWr was handed our open letter. Having received no response other than the empty words of the statement of the Conference of Rectors of Polish Universities, we declare the start of a peaceful, occupational strike. Seeing no other way forward, in solidarity with students in Poland and abroad (University of Warsaw and Jagiellonian University), we have decided to occupy the public space of the University. As an institution, it should represent the voice of the academic community, and not just it’s authorities. In light of the above, we call on the University of Wrocław to:
1. publicly, firmly and unequivocally condemn Israel's attack on the Gaza Strip and it’s occupation of Palestine,
2. immediately cease cooperation with Israeli academic institutions, research centers and other organizations and companies,
3. release all information concerning UWr’s cooperation with such institutions,
4. boycott Israeli institutions at the national and international level until the occupation of Palestine ends, recognize the right of Palestinians to equality and self-determination, and recognize the right of return for Palestinian refugees.
With the ever-present issue of anti-Semitism in mind, we stress that the fundamental value motivating our actions is an opposition to the oppression of any ethnic or racial minority. There is no consent on our part for any manifestation of racism, Islamophobia, anti-Semitism and Arabophobia. Never again for anyone.

DAY 15 (17.06.2024)

The week started off calm. We reorganized the patio – it’s now a big space for learning and hanging out together. We also put a second information point there, so that it’s easier to get familiar wiht the space of the occupation, and ask any questions you may have if you want to get involved in it. At 18:00 we used acrylic paints and a large sheet to make a massive keffiyeh.

DAY 14 (16.06.2024)

Today at 14:00 we held a sticker making workshop, organized by Sybil from @mykofanes.vlepy. Together we sat on the patio, chatted, painted stickers and listened to Sybil tell us about the process of making and printing the stickers. We always enjoy coming together to chat and spend time in a productive manner with people who are open to the ideas behind our occupation.

DAY 13 (15.06.2024)

Today we held the „March for Gaza”. We started at 13:00 at plac Uniwersytecki 1 under the Szermierz fountain, and ended at Plac Wolności. Along the way we passed through Szewska Street, where we gave a speech from the windows: We are here as the student community of the University of Wroclaw. We do not agree with the university’s passivity and it’s cooperation with institutions that support genocide. We are here because we are unable to be indifferent to the tragedy unfolding in Gaza and the West Bank. For 13 days now, we have been occupying, three rooms and the patio in the building of the Institute of Ethnology and Cultural Antropohlogy, Cultural Studies, and History, because the open letter with our demands was fully ignored. We wish to be heard, we want to engage in dialogue with the university authorities. So far we have only been met with attempts at intimidation, scaring us away from the occupied buildings, and obstructing our activities. We can not understand, and are immensely disappointeed with the indifference of the rector, who has been avoiding any direct contact with us. A lack of response to Israel’s actions is consent. We call for their immediate condemnation! We express solidarity with our fellow students in Warsaw, who, in order to increase their pressure on Alojzy Nowak, University of Warsaw’s rector, moved their occupation to the main campus. The university authorities decided to call for a police intervention against their own students. With force they broke up a peaceful demonstration and brought criminal charges against the students forward. We all fight against the killing of Palestinians, and for that we are treated as hoodlums. We call on the authorities of Polish universities to stop fighting their students, and start fighting the genocide! We are not going to go anywhere until our demands are met. Every day of inaction that goes by is another day of tacit consent to genocide. It is another day marked by the deaths of defenceless people. It is another day marked by the loss of more innocent lives. Never again for anyone.

DAY 12 (14.06.2024)

Today we held a meeting with Jo from the Nieczytelne collective, titled „From the Women’s Strikes to student occupations”. We spoke about the story, development and end of the Women’s Strikes. We analyzed these events in retrospect, trying to find constructive conclusions about their identity and legacy, and we, as protesters, can learn from them. Thank you all for the meeting, presence, and active participation in the discussion! We’d like to remind you all about the “March for Gaza”, which will be starting tomorrow at 13:00 under the Szermierz Fountain.

DAY 11 (13.06.2024)

Once again we have been visited by the rector’s envoy, who gave us a letter concerned with the illegality of the Day of Palestinian Culture we were organizing. We did not sign the document’s receipt, because we did not agree with it’s content – as stated in point 5 of the signed rules of our peaceful occupation, the protesters, as well as those invited by them have the right to freely enter and leave the occupied area. Under the provisions of the rules, we wanted to organize an array of events and meetings, inviting guests that could broaden our knowledge of the history and culture of Palestine. That day we also received an answer to our request for the release of information about our university’s cooperation with Israeli institutions, research centres, and companies. Due to the broad scope of our inquiry and the need to coordinate the acquisition of information from multiple faculties of the UWr, the rector has extended the deadline for said data’s publishing until the19th of July 2024. Nevertheless, the Day of Palestinian Culture continued as planned. At 15:00 we held a meeting with speeches about the event itself, as well as the protest. We were also visited by our friends, who came to us from the occupation of the Collegium Broscianum at the Jagiellonian University in Krakow. At 15:30 we held a reading of Palestinian poetry on the patio, which was attended by invitees and protesters. You can still find the poems we read in the occupied room number 9, next to other books and pamphlets. At 17:00 Charlie showed us how to prepare magloubeth – a traditional Arab dish, only in a vegetarian version. The dish is prepared in a pot that is flipped upside down when serving. That’s the reason for the name magloubeth, which when translated means “upside down”. At 18:00 we met our Palestinian friends living in Wrocław in room 4. The conversations about their lived experiences and Palestinian culture were so moving and engaging that we had to relocate outside the room, and the participants in the event expressed a desire to repeat it! At 19:00 we once again met with our friends from the Canadabkeh collective. We repeated the dabke choreography we learned at our last meeting that was especially prepared for us. Everyone involved enjoyed themselves during the event.

DAY 10 (12.06.2024)

Today our Palestinian friend, Charlie, organized our first lesson of the Arabic language. Sitting around a large table at the patio together, we learned basic phrases and words, and the Arabic alphabet. Meanwhile, preparations for the Day of Palestinian Culture were in full swing. At 22:54 last night we received a message from the dean of the Faculty of History and Pedagogy, Paweł Klint, that urged us to cancel the event due to a lack of permission to hold it. In response, we informed the dean that the event we were organizing was not a mass event, and instead only a way to make the day more engaging for the occupiers and the people invited by them, especially since the agreement, signed by both sides, states that we are allowed to issue invitations to the occupied area, as it is a public space. Moreover, we consider sending such a request so late in the day frivolous.

DAY 1 (03.06.2024)

Our peaceful sit-in protest began. We occupied three rooms and the patio in the building of the Institute of Cultural Studies, Ethnology and Anthropology at Szewska 50/51. We read the Manifesto of the Peaceful Sit-in Protest. Then – in the rhythm of the local Samba – we put up Palestine flags and banners with slogans that emphasize what we are fighting for. An hour later, we were visited by the representatives of the University of Wrocław. After almost two-hour negotiations we signed the agreement with the rules of our stay in the building. By virtue of the agreement, we were acknowledged as a representation of the part of the academic community that has a right to state demands. A week before – on May 27th – the open letter signed by more than 1300 people was given to the Rector. We met with the Rector and present him our demands for the University of Wrocław:
1) public, firm and clear-cut condemnation of Israeli attack on the Gaza Strip and occupation of Palestine,
2) immediate severance of relations with the Israeli academic institutions, centers of scholarship and other organizations and companies,
3) sharing the information about such relations and their extent,
4) boycott of Israeli institutions internally and internationally till the end of Palestine occupation, recognition of Palestinians right to equality and self-determination, recognition of the right to come back of Palestinian refugees.
In return, we were given a well-known statement of the Polish Rectors Conference, which we acknowledge as empty and unclear.
